About Faith Best

- A GIS Enthusiast with a Conservation Biology Background -

Hello, I'm Faith Best, a dedicated advocate for environmental conservation and a passion for Geographic Information Systems (GIS). With a solid foundation in Conservation Biology and a love for the natural world, I have embarked on a journey to harness these foundations to help fuel my work in GIS to help society and the environment work sustainably together.

My academic journey began at Fleming College where I developed a deep understanding of ecological principles and the importance of preserving the natural world. It was during my studies that I discovered the potential of GIS and a whole new world of analyzing and visualizing spatial data to address complex environmental challenges opened my eyes to a new career path.

Driven by my curiosity and passion for GIS, I pursued further studies and training in the field through Fleming College's GIS Program and working for the MNRF. Immersing myself in the learning opportunities and projects honed my skills in spatial analysis, remote sensing and cartography. Each project and learning experience has created a deeper enthusiasm for GIS and has empowered me to focus on a career in geospatial technology.

Throughout my experiences with GIS, I have had the privilege of applying GIS techniques to a diverse range of projects, starting with habitat suitability analysis to applying Acrpy coding to create a park suitability analysis in southern Ontario.

As I continue to expand my GIS portfolio, I am excited to share my journey and showcase the projects that I have found impactful in addressing real-world challenges. Join me as we navigate the incredible and dynamic possibilities of GIS technology and the opportunities to drive positive change.
